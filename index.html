Interest

Algebraic Geometry
  • We study algebraic geometry not only for its theoretical beauty but for the real world applications. For ethos, symplectic geometry derives some physics problems which derive complex geometry, which is quite algebraic geometric i.e. mirror symmetry and string theory. Second, Lie algebra was traditionally totally different subject from algebraic geometry, but turns out to be similar. Third, Non-commutative geometry is for quantum mechanics. F1 scheme is a monoidal scheme that can describe toric variety and K-theory, and from categorical perspective, it might be derived algebraic geometry.
  • Back in 1960s, Grothendieck's philosophy is the theoretical beauty of algebraic geometry, since he expanded the classical notion of algebraic variety to scheme to solve problems that classically could not e.g. étale topology for log scheme, étale homotopy group. Algebraic geometry for grand unified theory of mathematics.
Mirror Symmetry
  • Mirror symmetry is a correspondence of Hodge structures of geometries. For example, semi-infinite variation of Hodge structure defines Frobenius manifold for A-model and B-model resp, and they are isomorphic. Mirror symmetry origicinally arised from physics: A-model studies Gromov-Witten invariant that counts quantum instanton, and B-model studies Period integral for superconductivity.
  • Geometric Langlands program studies string theory. Geometric Satake correspondence is equivalence of representation category of algebraic group (Lie algebra) and category of D-modules (algebraic geometry). It describes N=4 Yang-Mill theory and type IIB string theory correspondence, that can be further enhanced by derived algebraic geometry. Other cool stuffs are SYZ conjecture, Higgs bundle etc.
  • ∞-dimensional Lie algebra is a tool which helps bridge partition function and A-model in mirror symmetry, so they can describe crystal melting. Still mirror symmetry.
Geometry and Dynamic System
  • We describe canonical/geometric/stochastic quantization with Lie algebraic technique (or Hopf algebra), while it's vice versa, we study classical theory from the quantum theory. We study classical thermodynamics from quantum mechanics i.e. Brownian motion (stochastic calculus) and entropy (ergodic theory).
  • AdS/CFT might be unifying the theory of quantum gravity, that has a surprising correspondence to 4-dimensional Navier Stokes Equation (NSE).

  • Satake Isomorphism (Dec 3 2022)
    Classical Satake isomorphism claims the spherical Hecke algebra (or double coset of algebraic group with p-adic number) has a correspondece with a representation ring of the algebraic group, and it was first formulated to prove Kazhdan-Lustzig formula. Geometric Satake isomorphism is its generalization, where naïvely we choose t-adic number in place of p-adic number to describe geometry, or less naïvely, correspondence of category of D-module of Hecke categories and category of representation of the algebraic group. Hecke category signifies open disk, which is why we call it "geometric". Geometric Satake isomorphism is used to study SYZ conjecture, homological mirror symmetry conjecture, Higgs bundle etc. Quantum Satake isomorphism relates representation of algebraic group to quantum cohomology(e.g. cohomology of Schubert cell) to study representation of spinor variety and Quadratic etc. This is for the talk in KU/KSU talk and Lie algebra(math814) final presentation.

  • Number theory with a view toward algebraic geometry (Oct 10 2022)
    There is no solution of quitic polynomial by roots of unity, namely, finite nonabelian Galois extension is not solvable, but it's solvable by infinite abelian Galois extension. Consider that any polynomial of rational coefficient has a factorization over finite field, the polynomial can be further factorized to the p-adic field(Hensel's lemma). Note that p-adic field is an infinite separable abelian extension of finite field, and this connects to Galois representation, so modular form and Elliptic curve, Shimura-Taniyama conjecture, Weil conjecture: this is the foundational problem toward defining scheme theory. In a more modern language, Grothendieck defined étale topology in scheme theory with a henselization of Zariski topology, and this helped to solve Weil conjecture by Deligne. This is for the talk for Graduate Student Seminar.
