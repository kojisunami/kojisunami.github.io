  • Foundation of Math and Essay-like Algebraic Geometry(Spring 2025)
    First of all, we'll discuss set theory from research perspective for algebraic geometry. Consider intersection/union in set theory develops intersection theory, if Euclidean topology is added. A simplicial set is a set theoretical argument. Hom set is a fine moduli space by categorical mean, or we may apply set theoretical quotient on it, and its opposition notion is representation, that is to find an object X to construct a functor Hom(X,-). Local isomorphism is paraphrase of etaleness, and surjective map is covering map or projection. Topologically, compactification is a criteria of integrability in real analysis, and also Poincare duality.
    Second is etymological perspective. Why do we discuss algebraic geometry, even though algebra and geometry are independent subjects.
    Lastly, category is exactly geometry itself, since fiber bundle consists of fiber(morphisms) and its base(objects). Assuming we've known sheaf theory, we'll construct stack, and how it will affect to moduli problem.
    Also, in foundation of math, the purpose of the expansion of naive set theory is to discuss a set of sets. Set of sets is actually very common in abstract/sigma algebra and category theory, hence AOC, filters, and Grothendieck universe have many applications.

  • Intro to Algebra, Geometry, not Algebraic Geometry(Spring 2025)
    Trace of a matrix is an invariant of Haar integral, partition function of QFT, Lagrangian, and that's representation theory (eigenvalue problem). Another linear algebra is tensor, Z-graded vector spaces, Boson/Fermion, classifying components by dimensions and degrees, For Lie groups, SO(3), Lorenzian, and spinors solves Schrödinger eq, QFT, AdS/CFT, and BPS state, that's also mathematicians' interests: These Lie groups are all rooted in su(2), and su(2) is easier because of complex number, and Heisenberg group for Symplectic geometry. In geometry, curvature is a gap of parallel transport defined by connection, and curvature enumerate holonomy, that's for Chern-Simon theory in QFT. Geometry also studies dimension and degree by intersection theory, and these invariants are better preserved in complex instead of real, Symplectic instead of Lagrangian resp, that's in particular, quantum cohomology and Floer homology. Finally, Hall algebra, Yang-Baxter, quiver, and supersymmetry, that's algebra other than homological algebra or algebraic geometry.

  • Intro to Homology and Category(Spring 2025)
    Homological algebra counts Euler characteristic and intersection theory (linearize and making it a finger counting problem), so it's a tool that counts geometry. Also, if the geometry has a complex structure, it might have Hodge structure, and if we treat Lie group/algebra valued function in symplectic geometry in physics, it might be BRST complex to calculate ghost field/Lagrangian in QFT. We will also explain vector bundle, that is geometric version of homological algebra, and the connection of vector bundle is a generalization of differential.
    We'll show why category theory is more powerful than abstract algebra. From the basic categorical definition constructs standard resolution. Presheaf is already an interesting definition, since functoriality better classifies functions than naive set theory (if a function has singular points, the domain will dynamically change), while contravariantness implies algebra(algebra) and coalgebra(geometry) duality. Sheaf theory systematically studies vector bundle, and category of coherent sheaves is abelian. The power of category theory generalizes sheaf to stack and moduli theory. In homological algebra, category of chain complexes is abelian, or derived category has triangulated and dg-structure, and rich in its structures. Finally, the purpose of homological algebra is to identify equivalence of categories, that's Morita equivalence and quasi-Frobenius.

  • Satake Isomorphism and Geometric Langlands(Dec 3 2022)
    Classical Satake isomorphism claims the spherical Hecke algebra (or double coset of algebraic group with p-adic number) has a correspondece with a representation ring of the algebraic group, and it was first formulated to prove Kazhdan-Lustzig formula. Geometric Satake isomorphism is its generalization, where naïvely we choose t-adic number in place of p-adic number to describe geometry, or less naïvely, correspondence of category of D-module of Hecke categories and category of representation of the algebraic group. Hecke category signifies open disk, which is why we call it "geometric". Geometric Satake isomorphism is used to study SYZ conjecture, homological mirror symmetry conjecture, Higgs bundle etc. Quantum Satake isomorphism relates representation of algebraic group to quantum cohomology(e.g. cohomology of Schubert cell) to study representation of spinor variety and Quadratic etc. This is for the talk in KU/KSU talk and Lie algebra(math814) final presentation.

  • Henselization and Etale cohomology (Oct 10 2022)
    There is no solution of quitic polynomial by roots of unity, namely, finite nonabelian Galois extension is not solvable, but it's solvable by infinite abelian Galois extension. Consider that any polynomial of rational coefficient has a factorization over finite field, the polynomial can be further factorized to the p-adic field(Hensel's lemma). Note that p-adic field is an infinite separable abelian extension of finite field, and this connects to Galois representation, so modular form and Elliptic curve, Shimura-Taniyama conjecture, Weil conjecture: this is the foundational problem toward defining scheme theory. In a more modern language, Grothendieck defined étale topology in scheme theory with a henselization of Zariski topology, and this helped to solve Weil conjecture by Deligne. This is for the talk for Graduate Student Seminar.
    my study note
